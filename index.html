Bullseye Chart

Bullseye chart made with the Raphael graphics lib. It supports mouse interaction, adding and removing points at runtime, serializing data points, and more.

Features

  • works seamlessly with Raphael: Raphael('canvas').bullseye()
  • easily serializable with polar coordinates
  • mouse interaction and callbacks: onMouseOver, onPointClick, onSliceClick
  • add / remove / drag points at runtime
  • works in Chrome, FF, and IE*
* works in IE9, briefly tested with IE7 and 8

Example

Bullseye chart illustrating WW2 battles chronologically. Greens are allied victories and reds are axis' victories. Size of dot represents casualties.

Hover over a point to see additional data.

Usage

    Raphael('canvas', 640, 600).bullseye({
        'sliceLabels': ['Pacific', 'Atlantic', 'Mediterranean'],
        'ringLabels': [1940, 1941, 1942, 1943, 1944, 1945]
    });

Check out the project page dimarr/raphael.bullseye.js for more complete documentation.
